My research stories

Health-care Digital Twins using AI and VR

Summer 2023 - PI: Prof. Dandan Zhang, University of Bristol

As the sun's warm embrace enveloped the world, I found myself standing on the precipice of a summer that would redefine my understanding of innovation and compassion. Guided by the brilliant mind of Professor Dandan Zhang, the realm of health-care digital twins using generative AI and virtual reality unfolded before me like a tapestry of possibilities.

Chapter 1: Crafting Lifelike Avatars

At the heart of this journey, I wielded the tools of wizards Blender and Maya to breathe life into digital avatars. Like a sculptor shaping clay, I meticulously molded each feature, every contour, until these virtual beings were uncannily lifelike. But this was just the beginning; I ventured further, learning the art of animation to infuse them with gestures that echoed human nuances.

Chapter 2: Conversations Beyond Reality

The ChatGPT API marked a portal to a new realm -- one where natural language processing intertwined with virtual existence. The avatars, now equipped with speech services, could converse, understand, and empathize. But we didn't stop there. Our innovation went deeper, birthing a memory mechanism that endowed these digital companions with contextually-aware responses. The avatars didn't just speak; they understood, fostering connections that felt undeniably human.

Chapter 3: The Symphony of Synergy

In Unity, the digital twins converged -- avatars, speech services, and advanced Language Models (LLMs) melded seamlessly. A symphony of technology orchestrated by human touch. These digital beings, once disparate entities, now stood as a united front, poised to revolutionize healthcare engagement. As users stepped into this world, they were greeted not by machines, but by companions; their journeys embraced in the arms of compassion and innovation.

Epilogue: A Summer to Remember

Summer 2023 was more than a sequence of months; it was an odyssey through realms of innovation and emotion. Under the tutelage of Professor Dandan Zhang, I discovered that technology wasn't just about advancement -- it was about extending the hands of empathy. Avatars ceased to be just pixels; they became confidants. Conversations transcended scripts; they became heartbeats. Digital twins weren't just creations; they were companions in the tapestry of human healing. This summer, I realized that innovation is more than circuits and algorithms; it's about crafting tools that amplify our humanity. And as the sun set in Summer 2023, its legacy lived on in avatars that smiled, conversations that resonated, and digital twins that held the promise of a compassionate future.
